  • My scientific research interests lie in global analysis and differential geometry, and their applications to hydrodynamics and shape analysis. Current projects include:

    • Conjugate points of L2 kinetic energy metrics on diffeomorphism groups and their application to stability in dynamic meteorology;
    • Generalising the BCH formula to diffeomorphism groups with a right-invariant metric;
    • The construction of geometric invariants of submanifolds which have applications to the statistical analysis of shapes, the modelling of probability distributions on manifolds, and hydrodynamics (joint work with Professor Stephen Marsland);
    • An exploration of alternative graph representations which are suitable for statistically analyzing populations of graphs and developing dimensionality reduction techniques (joint work with Dr. Anna Calissano and Professor Xavier Pennec);
    • Lagrangian and Eulerian stability of right-invariant metrics on (possibly infinite dimensional) Lie Groups and its relationship to uncertainty quantification of diffeomorphometry frameworks such as LDDMM.

  • My significant projects are

    • Knotted Solutions to the Euler Equations on 3-Sphere: We generalise the dirac delta distribution from singletons to closed curves in 3-space, and in such a way that it belongs to a Lebesgue class of differential forms. An important consequence of this is that it's essential support is of full measure yet can be shrunk into any given neighbourhood of the subset it models. As an application of this tool we use it to construct non-stationary solutions to the Euler Equations of hydrodynamics supported around any finite set of linked, but non-intersecting, knots -- our solutions are related several results in the literature, including the compactly supported Euler flows of Gavrilov and the vortex tubes of Peralta-Salas and Inciso.

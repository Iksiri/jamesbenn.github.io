  • My scientific research interests lie in global analysis and differential geometry, and their applications to hydrodynamics and shape analysis. Current projects include: (1) conjugate points of L2 kinetic energy metrics on diffeomorphism groups and their application to stability in dynamic meteorology; (2) generalising the BCH formula to diffeomorphism groups with a right-invariant metric (3) the construction of geometric invariants of submanifolds which have applications to the statistical analysis of shapes, the modelling of probability distributions on manifolds, and hydrodynamics (joint work with Professor Stephen Marsland); (4) an exploration of alternative graph representations which are suitable for statistically analyzing populations of graphs and developing dimensionality reduction techniques (joint work with Dr. Anna Calissano and Professor Xavier Pennec); and (5) Lagrangian and Eulerian stability of right-invariant metrics on (possibly infinite dimensional) Lie Groups and its relationship to uncertainty quantification of diffeomorphometry frameworks such as LDDMM.
